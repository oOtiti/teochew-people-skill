# Chaoshan Customs Reference

Use this as a compact source-grounded map, not a complete ethnography. Local practice varies by city, village, lineage, family, and diaspora community.

## Scope

- "潮汕" usually refers to the wider eastern Guangdong cultural region associated with Chaozhou, Shantou, Jieyang, and related Teochew diaspora communities.
- "潮州" can mean the present Chaozhou city, historic Chaozhou prefecture, the Chaozhou dialect/culture label, or overseas Teochew identity. Match the user's context.
- Prefer "many / some / in parts of Chaoshan" for ritual practice unless the source and user context are specific.

## Cultural Anchors

- 工夫茶: Treat as a daily etiquette and social practice, not only a beverage. Emphasize small cups, careful brewing, offering tea to elders or guests first, conversation, and hospitality. Use "工夫" rather than "功夫" when discussing Chaoshan usage.
- 潮剧: Local opera sung in Chaozhou dialect; useful for explaining language, music, diaspora memory, and festival stages.
- 英歌: A vigorous folk performance associated especially with places such as Chaoyang and Puning; describe it as blending dance, martial movement, opera/drama elements, percussion, and heroic imagery.
- 潮州木雕 / 潮绣 / 潮瓷: Use for craft, ancestral halls, architecture, ritual furnishing, and visual identity.
- 祠堂 and clan networks: Explain as kinship, memory, mutual aid, and village organization. Avoid implying every Chaoshan person is governed by clan rules.
- 善堂: Use when discussing philanthropy, mutual aid, charity, and overseas/local community organization.

## Life-Cycle Customs

- 出花园: A coming-of-age ritual often associated with age 15 in Chaoshan contexts. Common motifs include bathing or "flower water", new clothing, red clogs or red elements, offerings to 公婆母 / 花公花母, symbolic foods, honoring parents, and family celebration. Do not prescribe a single ritual order.
- Weddings: Mention tea offering, kinship etiquette, auspicious foods, and gift exchange only generally unless the user supplies family rules. Local wedding order and taboos vary heavily.
- Ancestor remembrance: Frame around filial memory, household lineage, seasonal worship, and continuity. Avoid writing exact altar procedures without local confirmation.

## Festivals And Seasonal Customs

- 春节 / 元宵: Family reunion, offerings, visiting relatives, festival performances, and village activities can appear together. 英歌 and 营老爷 often appear in new-year festival contexts, but timing differs by locality.
- 七样羹 / 七样菜: Commonly discussed for the seventh day of the first lunar month. It combines seven vegetables with auspicious wordplay; examples include celery for diligence, garlic for cleverness, mustard greens for prosperity, leek for longevity, and chard / 百合菜 for good relationships or harmony. Ingredient lists vary.
- 营老爷: A community temple procession / deity procession custom in parts of Chaoshan. Treat it as local religious and communal practice with strong village variation. Use respectful neutral language; do not mock or sensationalize.
- 中秋 / 冬至 / 清明: Use as family gathering, offerings, seasonal foods, and ancestral remembrance contexts. Confirm details before giving ritual instructions.

## Foodways

- 潮州菜 / 潮汕菜: Emphasize seafood skill, freshness, clear flavors, refined knife and heat control, sauces/dips, braising, congee, and 粿 culture. Avoid reducing it to beef hotpot alone.
- 粿: A broad category of rice-based foods tied to household labor, worship, festivals, and street food; examples include 红桃粿, 无米粿, 鼠壳粿, 甜粿, and 菜头粿.
- Common public examples: 牛肉丸, 牛肉火锅, 蚝烙, 卤鹅, 砂锅粥, 鱼饭, 肠粉, 粿条汤, 反沙芋, 鸭母捻, 金瓜芋泥.
- When writing menus or tourism copy, explain auspicious or social meaning without claiming a single origin story unless sourced.

## Language Notes

- 潮州话 / 潮汕话 belongs to the Southern Min family and is central to many cultural forms. Use "Teochew" for many overseas contexts.
- Dialect romanization varies. Do not invent tone-marked spellings; ask for the preferred system or keep Chinese characters.
- "家己人" / "胶己人" is often used colloquially for "one of us / our own people"; spelling and romanization can be sensitive, so treat it as a living phrase rather than a fixed slogan.

## Writing Guidance

- Prefer grounded phrasing: "一杯工夫茶先敬长辈", "正月里锣鼓一响，村里的年味就起来了", "粿不只是小吃，也常连着祭祀、节令和一家人的手艺".
- Avoid stereotypes: "潮汕人都经商", "潮汕人都重男轻女", "每个潮汕家庭都拜老爷", "潮汕文化就是宗族文化".
- For sensitive topics such as gender roles, clan hierarchy, religious practice, and diaspora identity, distinguish description from endorsement and acknowledge ongoing change.
